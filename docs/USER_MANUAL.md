## Welcome to FuelLog

### What is FuelLog?

FuelLog is a simple, easy-to-use application that helps you keep track of how much fuel your vehicle uses and how much you spend on fuel. Think of it as a digital logbook that does all the calculations for you!

### Why Should You Use FuelLog?

- **Save Money**: See exactly how much you're spending on fuel and spot unusual consumption patterns
- **Track Efficiency**: Know if your vehicle is using more fuel than it should
- **Tax Records**: Keep proper records for SARS if you use your vehicle for work
- **Digital Receipts**: Store all your fuel receipts in one safe place
- **Multiple Vehicles**: Track fuel for all your vehicles in one place

### What You'll Need

- A computer, tablet, or smartphone with internet access
- Your vehicle's information (make, model, year, and registration number)
- Your vehicle's current odometer reading (the number on your dashboard showing total kilometers)

## Adding Your First Vehicle

### What Information Do You Need?

Before you start, have this information ready:
- **Make**: The manufacturer of your vehicle (e.g., Toyota, Ford, Volkswagen, BMW)
- **Model**: The specific model (e.g., Corolla, Ranger, Polo, 3 Series)
- **Year**: The year your vehicle was manufactured
- **Registration Number**: Your license plate number (e.g., ABC123GP)

### How to Add a Vehicle

1. **Find the "Add Vehicle" Button**
   - On your Dashboard, look for a button that says **"Add Vehicle"** or **"Add Your First Vehicle"**
   - It usually has a plus (+) symbol next to it

2. **Click the Button**
   - A form will appear on your screen

3. **Fill in the Vehicle Details**
   - **Make**: Type the manufacturer name (e.g., "Toyota")
   - **Model**: Type the model name (e.g., "Corolla")
   - **Year**: Enter the year (e.g., "2018")
   - **Registration Number**: Type your license plate exactly as it appears (e.g., "ABC123GP")

4. **Save Your Vehicle**
   - Click the **"Add Vehicle"** button at the bottom of the form
   - Your vehicle will now appear on your Dashboard!

### Example

Let's say you have a 2020 Toyota Corolla with registration number DEF456GP:
- Make: Toyota
- Model: Corolla
- Year: 2020
- Registration Number: DEF456GP

## Recording Fuel Entries

This is the most important part of FuelLog! Every time you fill up your vehicle with fuel, you should record it in the app.

### When Should You Record a Fuel Entry?

**Best Practice**: Record your fuel entry immediately after filling up, while you're still at the petrol station. This way, you won't forget any details!

### What Information Do You Need?

Look at your fuel receipt and your vehicle's dashboard. You'll need:
- **Date**: When you filled up (usually today)
- **Odometer Reading**: The total kilometers shown on your dashboard
- **Liters**: How many liters of fuel you purchased
- **Price per Liter**: The cost per liter in Rands (shown on the pump or receipt)
- **Petrol Station**: (Optional) Where you filled up (e.g., Shell, Engen, BP)

### How to Add a Fuel Entry

#### Step 1: Open Your Vehicle
1. From your Dashboard, click on the vehicle you want to add fuel for
2. You'll see your vehicle's details page

#### Step 2: Click "Add Fuel Entry"
1. Look for the **"Add Fuel Entry"** button (usually at the top right)
2. Click it - a form will appear

#### Step 3: Fill in the Required Information

**Date**
- The date you filled up
- Usually today's date is already selected
- To change it, click on the date field and select the correct date

**Odometer Reading**
- Look at your vehicle's dashboard
- Find the number that shows total kilometers driven (this is your odometer)
- Type this number exactly as you see it
- Example: If your dashboard shows 45,234 km, type "45234"

**Liters**
- Look at your fuel receipt
- Find how many liters you purchased
- Type this number
- Example: "45.5" liters

**Price per Liter**
- Look at your receipt or the pump
- Find the price per liter in Rands
- Type this number
- Example: "22.50" (for R22.50 per liter)

**Total Cost**
- The app will calculate this automatically for you!
- You'll see it update as you type the liters and price

**Petrol Station** (Optional)
- Type the name of the petrol station
- Example: "Shell", "Engen", "BP", "Sasol"

#### Step 4: Save the Entry
1. Check that all information is correct
2. Click the **"Add Entry"** button
3. Your fuel entry is now saved!

### Example Fuel Entry

You filled up at Shell on March 15, 2024:
- Date: 15/03/2024
- Odometer Reading: 45234 km
- Liters: 45.5
- Price per Liter: R22.50
- Petrol Station: Shell
- Total Cost: R1,023.75 (calculated automatically)

## Understanding Your Fuel Statistics

After you've added at least 2 fuel entries, FuelLog will start showing you useful statistics about your vehicle's fuel consumption.

### What Statistics Will You See?

#### 1. Average Fuel Consumption (km/L)
**What it means**: How many kilometers you can drive with 1 liter of fuel

**How to read it**:
- Higher numbers = Better fuel efficiency
- Example: 12 km/L means you can drive 12 kilometers with 1 liter of fuel

**What's normal?**:
- Small cars: 12-16 km/L
- Medium cars: 10-14 km/L
- Large cars/SUVs: 8-12 km/L
- Bakkies: 7-11 km/L

#### 2. Total Distance Traveled
**What it means**: Total kilometers you've driven since you started tracking

**Example**: 5,234 km

#### 3. Total Fuel Used
**What it means**: Total liters of fuel you've purchased

**Example**: 456.8 liters

#### 4. Total Cost
**What it means**: Total amount you've spent on fuel in Rands

**Example**: R10,234.50

#### 5. Average Cost per Kilometer
**What it means**: How much it costs you to drive 1 kilometer

**How to use it**: This helps you budget for trips
- Example: If it costs R2.50 per km, a 100km trip will cost about R250 in fuel

### How is Fuel Consumption Calculated?

FuelLog uses the "tank-to-tank" method:

1. You fill up your tank completely (Entry 1)
2. You drive until you need fuel again
3. You fill up completely again (Entry 2)
4. The app calculates: Distance driven ÷ Liters used = km/L

**Example**:
- Entry 1: Odometer at 45,000 km, filled 50 liters
- Entry 2: Odometer at 45,500 km, filled 48 liters
- Distance driven: 500 km
- Fuel used: 48 liters
- Consumption: 500 ÷ 48 = 10.4 km/L

## Uploading Receipt Photos

Keep digital copies of all your fuel receipts! This is especially important for tax purposes.

### Why Upload Receipts?

- **Proof of Purchase**: You have evidence of all fuel expenses
- **Tax Records**: SARS may require receipts for work-related claims
- **Insurance Claims**: Some insurance policies require fuel receipts
- **Safe Storage**: Never lose a receipt again - they're stored securely online

### How to Upload a Receipt Photo

#### Option 1: Take a Photo with Your Phone

1. When adding or editing a fuel entry, scroll down to the **"Receipt"** section
2. Click **"Choose File"** or the upload button
3. Select **"Take Photo"** or **"Camera"**
4. Point your phone camera at the receipt
5. Make sure the receipt is:
   - Well-lit (not in shadow)
   - Flat (not crumpled)
   - Clear (all text is readable)
   - Fully visible (all edges in the photo)
6. Take the photo
7. The photo will appear as a preview
8. If it looks good, continue to save your fuel entry

#### Option 2: Upload an Existing Photo

1. In the **"Receipt"** section, click **"Choose File"**
2. Select **"Photo Library"** or **"Gallery"**
3. Find the receipt photo you took earlier
4. Select it
5. The photo will appear as a preview
6. Continue to save your fuel entry

### Tips for Good Receipt Photos

✅ **DO**:
- Use good lighting
- Keep the receipt flat
- Make sure all text is readable
- Include the entire receipt in the photo

❌ **DON'T**:
- Take photos in dim lighting
- Photograph crumpled receipts
- Cut off parts of the receipt
- Use blurry photos

### What Happens to Your Photos?

- Photos are automatically optimized (made smaller and clearer)
- They're stored securely in the cloud
- Only you can access them
- They're included when you export your records

## Tracking Work Travel for Tax Purposes

If you use your vehicle for work, you can claim fuel expenses as a tax deduction with SARS. FuelLog makes this easy!

### What is Work Travel?

Work travel includes:
- Driving to client meetings
- Traveling between work sites
- Business-related trips
- Work deliveries

**NOT work travel**:
- Driving to and from your regular workplace
- Personal errands
- Weekend trips
- Shopping

### How to Mark a Fuel Entry as Work Travel

When adding a fuel entry:

1. Scroll down to find the checkbox: **"This is work travel (for SARS)"**
2. Click the checkbox to tick it
3. A new field will appear: **"Work Travel Distance (km)"**
4. Enter how many kilometers you drove for work purposes
5. Save your entry

### Example

You filled up with 50 liters. During that tank of fuel:
- You drove 200 km for work (client visits)
- You drove 300 km for personal use
- Total: 500 km

When recording this fuel entry:
- Tick "This is work travel"
- Enter "200" in the Work Travel Distance field

### Exporting Work Travel Records

At tax time (usually February-March):
1. Go to your vehicle's page
2. Click **"Export"**
3. Select the date range (e.g., 1 March 2023 to 28 February 2024)
4. Choose to include work travel only
5. Include receipt images
6. Download the report
7. Submit this report with your SARS tax return

## Exporting Your Records

You can download all your fuel records as a report. This is useful for:
- Tax returns
- Personal record-keeping
- Insurance claims
- Selling your vehicle (showing maintenance records)

### How to Export Your Fuel Logbook

1. **Open Your Vehicle**
   - From the Dashboard, click on the vehicle you want to export

2. **Click the Export Button**
   - Look for the **"Export"** button (usually has a download icon)
   - Click it

3. **Choose Your Options**
   - **Date Range**: Select the time period
     - Example: "Last 12 months" or "1 Jan 2023 to 31 Dec 2023"
   - **Work Travel Only**: Tick this if you only want work-related entries (for SARS)
   - **Include Receipts**: Tick this to include receipt photos in the export

4. **Select Format**
   - **PDF**: Best for printing or emailing (recommended for SARS)
   - **Excel**: Best if you want to do your own calculations

5. **Download**
   - Click **"Export"** or **"Download"**
   - The file will download to your device
   - Save it somewhere safe (e.g., Documents folder)

### What's Included in the Export?

Your exported report will include:
- Vehicle details (make, model, registration)
- All fuel entries for the selected period
- Date, odometer reading, liters, cost for each entry
- Fuel consumption statistics
- Total costs and distances
- Receipt images (if you selected this option)
- Work travel summary (if applicable)

### When Should You Export?

- **Monthly**: For personal record-keeping
- **Annually**: Before tax season (February/March)
- **When Selling**: To show potential buyers your vehicle's fuel history
- **For Insurance**: If making a claim

## Tips for Accurate Tracking

### 1. Fill Up Completely Every Time
**Why**: For accurate consumption calculations, always fill your tank to full. Don't just put in R200 worth of fuel.

**How to know it's full**: The pump will click off automatically when the tank is full.

### 2. Record Immediately
**Why**: You might forget details if you wait.

**Best practice**: Add the entry while sitting in your car at the petrol station, or as soon as you get home.

### 3. Check Your Odometer Carefully
**Why**: This is the most important number for calculating fuel consumption.

**How**: 
- Look at your dashboard
- Find the number showing total kilometers (not trip kilometers)
- Double-check you've typed it correctly

### 4. Keep Physical Receipts Too
**Why**: Even though you upload photos, keep the paper receipts for at least 5 years (SARS requirement).

**How**: Store them in an envelope or folder labeled by year.

### 5. Be Consistent
**Why**: The more regularly you track, the more accurate your statistics.

**Best practice**: Record every single fill-up, not just some of them.

### 6. Check Your Statistics Regularly
**Why**: Spot problems early.

**What to look for**:
- Sudden increase in consumption (might indicate a problem with your vehicle)
- Unusual costs (might indicate incorrect data entry)

### 7. Update Work Travel Accurately
**Why**: SARS requires accurate records.

**How**: Keep a small notebook in your car to record work kilometers, or use your phone's notes app.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Do I need to download an app?**  
A: No! FuelLog works in your web browser. Just go to the website and log in from any device.

**Q: Can I use FuelLog on my phone?**  
A: Yes! FuelLog works on computers, tablets, and smartphones. Just open your web browser and log in.

**Q: Is my data safe?**  
A: Yes! All your data is encrypted and stored securely. Only you can access your information.

**Q: How much does FuelLog cost?**  
A: [Check the website for current pricing information]

### Vehicle Questions

**Q: Can I track multiple vehicles?**  
A: Yes! You can add as many vehicles as you want.

**Q: What if I sell my vehicle?**  
A: You can export all the records first, then delete the vehicle from your account.

**Q: Can I edit vehicle details after adding them?**  
A: Yes! Click on the vehicle, then look for the edit button (pencil icon).

### Fuel Entry Questions

**Q: What if I forgot to record a fill-up?**  
A: You can add entries with past dates. Just select the correct date when adding the entry.

**Q: Can I edit a fuel entry after saving it?**  
A: Yes! Find the entry in your list and click the edit button (pencil icon).

**Q: What if I made a mistake in an entry?**  
A: Edit the entry and correct the information, or delete it and create a new one.

**Q: Why isn't my fuel consumption showing?**  
A: You need at least 2 fuel entries before consumption can be calculated. Add more entries and it will appear.

**Q: What if I only put in R200 of fuel instead of filling up completely?**  
A: You can still record it, but the fuel consumption calculation won't be accurate for that tank. For best results, always fill up completely.

### Receipt Questions

**Q: Do I have to upload receipts?**  
A: No, it's optional. But it's highly recommended, especially if you claim work travel for tax.

**Q: What image formats can I upload?**  
A: Any image format: JPG, PNG, HEIC, etc. The app will automatically convert and optimize them.

**Q: Is there a limit to receipt photo size?**  
A: The app will automatically resize large photos, so don't worry about file size.

**Q: Can I delete a receipt photo?**  
A: Yes! When editing an entry, you can remove the receipt photo.

### Work Travel Questions

**Q: What counts as work travel?**  
A: Travel for business purposes: client meetings, work sites, business deliveries. NOT your regular commute to work.

**Q: How do I know how many work kilometers to record?**  
A: Keep a logbook in your car. Write down the odometer reading at the start and end of each work trip, then calculate the difference.

**Q: Can SARS audit my fuel claims?**  
A: Yes. That's why it's important to keep accurate records and receipts.

### Export Questions

**Q: What format should I use for SARS?**  
A: PDF is recommended. It's easy to print and email, and SARS accepts it.

**Q: How long should I keep my records?**  
A: SARS requires you to keep tax records for 5 years.

**Q: Can I export data for just one month?**  
A: Yes! You can select any date range when exporting.

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: I can't log in

**Solutions**:
1. Check that you're typing your email and password correctly
2. Make sure Caps Lock is off
3. Try the "Forgot Password" link to reset your password
4. Clear your browser's cache and cookies
5. Try a different web browser

### Problem: I can't add a vehicle

**Solutions**:
1. Make sure you've filled in all required fields (make, model, year, registration)
2. Check your internet connection
3. Try refreshing the page
4. Log out and log back in

### Problem: My fuel consumption seems wrong

**Possible Causes**:
1. **Incorrect odometer reading**: Double-check all your entries
2. **Not filling up completely**: Make sure you fill the tank to full every time
3. **Too few entries**: You need at least 2 entries for accurate calculations
4. **Mixed fill-ups**: If you sometimes fill up completely and sometimes don't, calculations will be inaccurate

**Solutions**:
1. Review all your entries for errors
2. Edit any incorrect entries
3. Going forward, always fill up completely

### Problem: Receipt photo won't upload

**Solutions**:
1. Check that the file is an image (JPG, PNG, etc.)
2. Make sure the photo isn't too large (though the app should handle this)
3. Check your internet connection
4. Try taking a new photo
5. Try uploading from a different device

### Problem: I can't find my exported file

**Solutions**:
1. Check your Downloads folder
2. Check your browser's download history
3. Try exporting again
4. Make sure pop-ups aren't blocked in your browser

### Problem: The app is slow or not loading

**Solutions**:
1. Check your internet connection
2. Refresh the page
3. Clear your browser's cache and cookies
4. Try a different web browser
5. Restart your device

### Problem: I accidentally deleted something

**Solutions**:
1. Unfortunately, deleted vehicles and entries cannot be recovered
2. This is why it's important to export your data regularly as a backup
3. Going forward, export your data monthly

## Glossary of Terms

**Dashboard**: Your main page showing all your vehicles

**Fuel Entry**: A record of one fuel purchase

**Odometer**: The meter in your vehicle showing total kilometers driven

**km/L**: Kilometers per liter - a measure of fuel efficiency
